SCOPE OF THE CONFERENCE

The field of mechanical engineering continues to evolve rapidly, driven by advancements in technology, materials science, and innovative design techniques. With the growing demand for sustainable solutions, automation, and cutting-edge manufacturing processes, it is essential to create a platform that brings together experts, researchers, and practitioners to exchange knowledge and collaborate on the future of mechanical engineering. This proposal outlines the plan to host an International Conference on Advances in Mechanical Engineering and Emerging Technology -2026 (ICAMEET- 2026), aimed at fostering global dialogue, sharing the latest research, and exploring the applications of modern mechanical engineering.

  • To provide a platform for researchers, engineers, and industry professionals to discuss recent developments and challenges in mechanical engineering.
  • To promote the exchange of ideas and innovations in mechanical design, automation, robotics, material science, energy systems, and manufacturing technologies.
  • To facilitate networking opportunities that could lead to future collaborations, partnerships, and research initiatives.
  • To address key global issues such as sustainability, energy efficiency, and automation through the lens of mechanical engineering.

Target Audience:

The event is designed to bring together a diverse group of participants, including academicians, researchers, and students from universities and research institutions who are engaged in advancing knowledge in mechanical engineering. It also welcomes industry professionals, engineers, and technologists from key sectors such as automotive, aerospace, energy, robotics, manufacturing, and thermal engineering, providing a platform for knowledge exchange and collaboration. Additionally, the event aims to attract policymakers, government officials, and corporate executives who are keen on fostering innovation and shaping the future of mechanical engineering. Anyone with a genuine interest in exploring the latest trends, technologies, and challenges in the field is encouraged to participate and contribute to the discourse.

  • Academicians, researchers, and students from universities and research institutions.
  • Industry professionals, engineers, and technologists from various sectors, including automotive, aerospace, energy, robotics, manufacturing and thermal engineering.
  • Policy makers, government officials, and corporate executives with an interest in mechanical engineering innovations.
  • Anyone interested in exploring the latest trends and challenges in the field

Key Themes and Topics

Track-1: Material Synthesis for environmental remediation, Energy storage and Conversion

Synthesis and characterization of materials, innovation and mechanistic features for sustainable chemical production, nanomaterials for environmental remediation, Advanced Oxidation processes (Electrochemical oxidation, catalytic oxidation, photocatalysis, solar treatment, etc.), Conversion of CO2 into valuable chemicals, Microplastics: Challenges and Remediation, Energy Harvesting, Battery Technology, Supercapacitors, Electrochemical Storage, Solar Energy Conversion, Thermoelectric Materials, Hydrogen Storage, Catalysis for Energy Applications, Solid-State Batteries, Sustainable Energy Solutions, Waste to resource recovery.

Track-2: Advance Manufacturing in Industry 4.0/5.0

Nanofabrication, Additive Manufacturing, 3D Printing, Smart Manufacturing, Process Optimization, Materials Processing, Quality Control, In-situ Characterization, Data-Driven Manufacturing, Sustainable Manufacturing, Scale-Up Processes, Novel principles/approaches for manufacturing, Composites (Polymer/metal/ceramics), Advanced Welding Technologies, Application of AI/ML in Manufacturing.

Track-3: Thermal Engineering

Combustion, Multiphase flow, Fluid mechanics, Heat and Mass transfer, Nano fluids thermal applications, Refrigeration, Solar energy, Thermal and fluid engineering applications, Air powered cars, Alternative fuels, CFD, Computational heat transfer, Heat exchanger, Heat transfer, Nano-and micro-technology for energy, Phase change materials, Renewable energy, Thermal management.

Track-4:Energy Development and Power Generation

Excitation systems, Power system stabilizers, Advanced and renewable energy technologies, Station design, operations and control, Modelling, simulation and control of power plants (electrical aspects), Monitoring and instrumentation of power plants (electrical aspects), Control of distributed generation, Hydroelectric power plants (electrical aspects), International practices in energy development, Solar/photovoltaic, Wind, Biomass, Batteries, Magneto hydrodynamics, Fuel cells, Superconducting magnetic energy storage, Flywheels, mechanical, hydraulic energy storage, Distributed storage, Industrial/commercial energy conservation, Grid interconnection, Tidal/wave power, Other.
